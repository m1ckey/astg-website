AEROSPACE TEAM GRAZ

Our Mission

The Aerospace Team Graz is a student team, where rocket and space enthusiasts from different fields of study and universities in Graz get together to work on different space related projects. The main projects deal with rockets to take part in international competitions. In addition, the team works on various propulsion systems, test beds and small scaled satellites.

One of the key aspects of the ASTG is to get hands-on experience in combining theory and practice to complement the various university studies. We accomplish this by researching and developing all the systems of our projects in house. It´s another great opportunity to write the Bachelor or Master thesis about topics the ASTG is dealing with.

While not only building rockets but also friendships, the ASTG offers the perfect environment for students to practice soft skills, get experience in teamwork and team lead and extend their network in the aerospace sector to create a bright future for humankind.

With this in mind: "The Sky is no longer the Limit!"

History

In October 2019, 13 students came together for the first time with the vision in mind to build rockets and take part in international competitions. The keystone was laid. The non-profit organization was founded and the first project, a prototype rocket called DODO, was defined.

When the ASTG got the possibility to take part in the European Rocketry Challenge in 2021, to fulfil the requirements, it was decided to let go DODO and focus all the power and knowledge on a new project, the first competition rocket AVES.
‍
Since the founding of the association, the interdisciplinary team grew to about 75 members from 4 different universities in Graz with more than 15 different studies.

Achievements

Although the Aerospace Team is a very young student team, it can already look back on several successes:

EuRoC 21: Technical Award
In 2021, the ASTG developed their first competition rocket AVES in just 4 months to participate in the European Rocketry Challenge (EuRoC). The Team managed to win the “Technical Award” for the best documentation of their project.
‍
EuRoC 22: New Space Award & Team Award
The participation at the EuRoC 2022 with their successor rocket AVES II was even more successful and was rewarded with two prizes. The "New Space Award" honours the best jury pitch of the project and the team's presentation as a launch service provider. For the "Team Award" the student team could convince with reliability, teamwork, organization and communication. In addition, ASTG was mentioned for the “Technical Award” as one of the best teams in this category.
